Today's Reading List 2026-07-22

$30 billion. That's the ceiling the Space Force just set for rocket launches — the same week a surgical robotics company that raised $300 million shut its doors for good. Money moves fast in this business. Staying power doesn't.

The Space Force wants to buy up to $30 billion in rocket launches. The Trump administration is asking the branch to do more, and more means more launch contracts stacking up behind SpaceX, Space Force's usual go-to. Worth watching who else gets a slice. https://arstechnica.com/space/2026/07/the-space-force-is-now-seeking-to-buy-up-to-30-billion-in-rocket-launches/

Vicarious Surgical raised about $300 million and just shut down anyway. Most of that came from a SPAC merger back in 2021 — the kind of money that looks bulletproof on a slide deck. It wasn't. Capital doesn't build a working product; it just buys time to try. https://www.therobotreport.com/vicarious-surgical-officially-shutting-down/

New Orleans didn't flood in 2005 because Katrina was strong. It flooded because of a bizarre design flaw baked into the levee system itself — Katrina was one of the hardest-hitting hurricanes ever to reach a US coastline, but the failure traces back to the engineering, not just the wind. Worth the read if you think "built to code" means "built right." https://practical.engineering/blog/2026/7/7/the-bizarre-flaw-in-the-new-orleans-levees

Tearing down a bridge is its own construction project. The old Iowa-Illinois Memorial Bridge over the Mississippi came down after its replacement opened in 2022 — two new bridges went up, and the old one had to come apart safely over an active river. Sequencing a demolition next to live traffic is its own trade. https://practical.engineering/blog/2026/6/16/how-to-demolish-a-bridge

A 30-meter rocket launched from a boat off Shanghai this week. Orienspace's Gravity-1, a solid rocket, put nine satellites into orbit in its third flight — sea launch skips the fixed pad entirely. No idea what their turnaround time looks like yet, but launching without real estate is a different kind of infrastructure problem. https://spacenews.com/gravity-1-sea-launch-off-shanghai-puts-9-satellites-into-orbit/
