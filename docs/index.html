Today's Reading List 2026-07-24

$1.7 billion raised for a robotics startup that hasn't built a single machine yet. The guy who wrote the check invented an app that replaced dispatchers — now he wants metal to replace muscle.

Travis Kalanick, Uber's co-founder, raised $1.7B for a new robotics startup called ATOMS. He says the goal is physical automation across industry — no product shown yet, just the capital and the ambition. Same playbook as ride-share: find the labor bottleneck, build something to route around it. Worth watching what ATOMS actually ships, because $1.7B buys a lot of slide decks before it buys a working robot. https://www.therobotreport.com/uber-co-founder-raises-1-7b-for-new-robotics-startup-atoms/

$105 million for a humanoid that rolls instead of walks. Holiday Robotics raised the round for FRIDAY, a wheeled humanoid with an upper body built for dexterous manipulation and hot-swappable batteries. Swap the battery, not the robot — that's the whole pitch. No downtime charging on a job that doesn't stop for lunch. https://www.therobotreport.com/holiday-robotics-raises-105m-wheeled-humanoid-friday/

Cheaper labor and cheaper materials both sound like the fix for construction costs. Brian Potter's latest in the productivity series says economies of scale are hard to capture in homebuilding — building bigger or cheaper doesn't automatically make building faster.
